Hydraulic Fluid Tank Inspection Guide

1. Verify fluid level is between MIN and MAX marks.
2. Inspect tank cap seal for cracks or looseness.
3. Listen for high-pitched whine near pump inlet; this may indicate cavitation.
4. Check suction line for air leaks, abrasion, or kinks.
5. Confirm return filter differential pressure is within service limits.
6. Inspect hoses and fittings for seepage around crimps and joints.
7. If fluid appears milky, test for water contamination and replace fluid.
8. After maintenance, run system for 2 minutes and re-check level and leaks.
